Factors influencing the ranking:

  • Play Money Balance: Accumulating more play money might contribute positively to a player's rank. It could signify experience and success within the play money games.
  • Game Performance: Winning games or tournaments, placing high in leaderboards, or consistently performing well can positively impact a player's rank.
  • Activity and Engagement: Regularly participating in games, staying active on the platform, and contributing positively to the community might also factor into the ranking algorithm.
  • Skill Level: Some systems might assess a player's skill level based on gameplay, strategies employed, or even through special skill-based challenges. This could influence the ranking as well.

How the ranking tiers are structured:

  • Basic Tiers: Newcomers or less active players might be in the lower tiers.
  • Intermediate Tiers: Players with moderate play money balances and reasonable game performance might fall into these tiers.
  • Advanced Tiers: These tiers could include players with substantial play money balances, consistent wins, high leaderboard rankings, or notable skill levels.
  • Elite Tiers: Reserved for the top players with significant play money, exceptional skills, and outstanding achievements on the platform.

Advantages of higher ranks:

Players in higher ranks might enjoy various perks, such as: Access to exclusive tournaments or games. Special badges or avatars to showcase their rank. Priority support or customer service. Recognition within the community.

Transparency and Fairness:

The ranking system should ideally be transparent, with clear criteria for advancement. It should also strive to be fair, rewarding skill and activity rather than just play money accumulation. In essence, while play money balance could be a contributing factor, a comprehensive ranking system in a poker website based on play money should consider various aspects of a player's performance and engagement to determine their rank.
